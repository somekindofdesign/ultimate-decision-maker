How to play

Beat your opponent!

Choose which hand to play in order to beat your opponent's hand.

Hands are revealed at the same time so pick the hand you think has the best change of winning.

If you win then the decision is made in your favour. But if your opponent wins then suck it up and do the right thing!

What beats what?

Choose two hands to see what the result would be before you play for real.

History of the Game

Created in 1998 by Sam Kass and Karen Bryla, Rock Paper Scissors Lizard Spock is based off the traditional Chinese game Rochambeau.

It's more commonly known in Western countries as Rock Paper Scissors and not only is it a bit of fun, it has been used as an objective decision making tool for a very long time.

The popular sitcom 'The Big Bang Theory' helped to popularise Kass and Bryla's modern version.
